Our Goals

Real estate goals come in all sorts of shapes and sizes, so don’t let anyone tell you yours are too big or small. That said, the best goals are those that move your business forward at a pace you are comfortable with. Small goals that can move things forward, for example, can be anything from driving by a predetermined amount of PG each month or talking to five new contacts a week. While relatively simple, these goals can have a lasting impact on the path your career takes. If you are more ambitious, however, you may be interested in setting larger goals. Since “larger” is a relative term, it may reference driving to more houses or even take place over longer periods of time. Nonetheless, the truth remains: Regardless of how big or small your goals are, the most important thing is that they move the needle.

Specific Real Estate Goals Every real estate business goal you set should be as specific as possible. Clearly define every term within the goal and establish actionable steps to follow. If you want to improve this year’s revenue, your goal should be more than “I want to increase my profits.” Alternatively, try “I want to increase this year’s profits so that I can increase next year’s marketing budget. I will start by evaluating my lead generation strategies and streamlining my systems. I will need the help of this employee and this mentor to make this goal a reality.” While not necessary, it can help create a physical checklist for yourself and cross out each stage of the goal as you accomplish it.
